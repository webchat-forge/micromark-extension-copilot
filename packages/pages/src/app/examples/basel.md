# Basel Problem

The Basel problem, solved by Euler in 1735, proves that:

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}
$$

## Historical Context

This problem stumped mathematicians for decades until Euler's brilliant solution. The general form for even powers is:

\[
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^{2k}} = \frac{(-1)^{k+1}B_{2k}(2\pi)^{2k}}{2(2k)!}
\]

Where B₂ₖ are the Bernoulli numbers.

## Related Series

1. **Alternating Series**
   \[
   \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{12}
   \]

2. **Higher Powers**
   $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^4} = \frac{\pi^4}{90} $$
   $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^6} = \frac{\pi^6}{945} $$
