# Euler's Identity

One of the most beautiful equations in mathematics:

\[
e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0
\]

This equation connects five fundamental mathematical constants:
- e (Euler's number)
- i (imaginary unit)
- π (pi)
- 1 (multiplicative identity)
- 0 (additive identity)

## Complex Exponentials

The general form of Euler's formula:
$$ e^{ix} = \cos(x) + i\sin(x) $$

When x = π:
\[
\begin{align*}
e^{i\pi} &= \cos(\pi) + i\sin(\pi) \\\\
&= -1 + i(0) \\\\
&= -1
\end{align*}
\]

## Applications

1. **Trigonometric Identities**
   Using Euler's formula:
   \[
   \cos(x) = \frac{e^{ix} + e^{-ix}}{2}
   \]
   \[
   \sin(x) = \frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2i}
   \]
